About

I am Hyun-Jun Jeon (전현준), an M.S. candidate in Aerospace Engineering at the Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST). As a member of the Space Structures & H/W Systems Lab, my research focuses on deployable structures, origami-inspired design, and reconfigurable systems for future space habitats and large-scale orbital infrastructure.


My foundation in Mechanical Engineering from Korea University (2019–2025) shaped my approach to engineering design and structural mechanics. During my undergraduate years, I developed hands-on skills through design competitions and a research internship in soft robotics at Seoul National University's BioRobotics Lab. I have also collaborated with industry partners — Hanwha Systems and LIG Nex1 — on satellite structural analysis and launch load assessment.


Alongside my research, I pursue creative work in making, drawing, and photography. I believe that curiosity and creativity are as essential to engineering as technical rigor.

Creative Mechanical Design — Best Design Award

Spring 2023 · Korea University, Department of Mechanical Engineering

Designed and built a gripper-based locomotion robot tasked with traversing a series of aluminum bars. Led the mechanical design in Fusion 360, fabricated components using a 3D printer, and implemented motor control via Arduino UNO with a linear actuator and MG90 servo motors. The project received the Best Design Award from the department.

Capstone Design — Mech Anemone II

Fall 2023 · Inter-University Capstone Design Fair

Represented the Korea University Mechanical Engineering department at an inter-university Capstone Design Fair. Extended the Creative Mechanical Design project into a construction-site gripper robot — "Mech Anemone II" — inspired by sea anemone tentacles, designed to handle structural loads such as beams and pipes.

Industry Collaboration — Satellite Structural Analysis

2025 – Present · Hanwha Systems & LIG Nex1

Participated in collaborative projects with Hanwha Systems and LIG Nex1, gaining practical experience in launch load analysis and structural analysis of satellites and aerospace modules. Developed skills in project scheduling and cross-disciplinary communication with industry engineers.

HASEL Actuator Research — BioRobotics Lab (BRL)

Dec. 2023 – Feb. 2024 · Seoul National University

Participated in the Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program (UROP) at the SNU BioRobotics Lab. Research focused on the fabrication and application of HASEL (Hydraulically Amplified Self-Healing Electrostatic) actuators — a soft actuator type that uses high-voltage breakdown to generate Maxwell stress for linear actuation. Successfully fabricated and tested HASEL actuators and explored a bio-inspired inching locomotion application. Used AutoCAD for structural layout design and laser cutting of electrode films.
